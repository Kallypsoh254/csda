Who We Are

A community shaped by grace.

CukSDA is the official Seventh-day Adventist student and staff community at the Cooperative University of Kenya, Karen Campus in Nairobi. We exist to connect the CUK community with the love, truth, and hope found only in Jesus Christ.

We are a multigenerational, multicultural fellowship — students, faculty, staff, and friends — all walking together in faith. Whether you are a lifelong Adventist or exploring Christianity for the first time, you are deeply welcome here.

Our church life is built around the Sabbath experience — from Sabbath School at 9:00 AM to the Divine Service at 11:00 AM — but our community extends through the whole week with prayer meetings, Bible studies, ministry projects, and campus outreach.

Purpose & Direction

Why we exist and where we're going.

Our Vision

A transformed campus community reflecting Christ.

We envision every student and staff member at CUK having a genuine, personal encounter with Jesus Christ — and a faith community strong enough to sustain and support that journey through university life and beyond.

Our Mission

Making disciples who worship, grow, and serve.

CukSDA exists to make and mature disciples of Jesus Christ within the CUK community — through authentic worship, intentional discipleship, compassionate service, and bold, Spirit-led evangelism to all people.

Our Commitment

Scripture-rooted, Spirit-led, community-driven.

Everything we do is grounded in the inspired Word of God, empowered by the Holy Spirit, and lived out in the context of a loving, accountable, diverse church family. We are proudly part of the global Seventh-day Adventist Church.

Our History

A journey of faith across the years.

From a small prayer group to a growing campus church family — here is how God has moved among us since the very beginning.

2008

The Seed Is Planted

A handful of Adventist students at CUK began meeting informally for prayer and Bible study in a dormitory common room. These early gatherings laid the spiritual foundation for what would become a thriving church community.

Foundation
2011

Official Recognition

CukSDA was officially registered as a campus ministry and recognised by both the university administration and the East Kenya Union Conference of Seventh-day Adventists. Sabbath services moved to the Main Hall for the first time.

Milestone
2014

Ministry Expansion

The Ambassadors for Christ Youth (AY) Society, the Health Ministry, and the Music Department were formally established, enabling the community to serve students with gifts across worship, wellness, and outreach.

2017

Our First Campus Evangelistic Series

CukSDA hosted its inaugural week-long campus evangelistic crusade in partnership with the East Kenya Union. The series saw over 40 students give their hearts to Christ and be baptised, a landmark moment for the community.

Growth
2020

Resilience Through the Pandemic

When COVID-19 disrupted in-person gatherings, CukSDA pivoted swiftly to online Sabbath services, virtual prayer nights, and digital Bible study groups — keeping the community connected and spiritually alive through one of the most challenging periods in modern history.

2022

Community Health & Wellness Initiative

Launching the CukSDA Health Ministry in full, the community partnered with local hospitals to offer free health screenings, nutrition workshops, and mental health support for students — serving over 300 people in its first year.

Today

Growing Stronger Together

With over 200 active members, eight thriving ministries, and a heart for the whole CUK campus community, CukSDA continues to grow in depth and breadth — always anchored in Christ, always open to all.

Core Values

What shapes everything we do.

These are not just words on a wall — they are the convictions that guide how we worship, relate, serve, and grow together as a campus church family.

01

Scripture First

The Bible is our final authority in all matters of faith, life, and doctrine. We read it, preach it, study it, and live it — believing every word is breathed out by God.

02

Authentic Worship

We believe worship is a whole-life response to God. We gather on the Sabbath with reverence, joy, and expectation — offering back to God the best of who we are.

03

Genuine Community

Nobody walks alone. We are committed to building deep, honest, and caring relationships — a family where every person is known, loved, and belongs from day one.

04

Intentional Discipleship

Faith grows in relationship. We invest intentionally in mentorship, Bible study, and one-on-one care — because we are called to make disciples, not just attendees.

05

Bold Evangelism

The good news of Jesus is the best news the world has ever heard. We share it boldly, compassionately, and creatively — on campus, in Karen, and beyond Kenya.

06

Wholistic Health

We believe the body, mind, and spirit are all sacred. CukSDA champions total wellbeing — nurturing physical health, emotional resilience, and spiritual wholeness in every member.

What We Believe

Anchored in Scripture, united in truth.

As a Seventh-day Adventist congregation, our beliefs are drawn from the 28 Fundamental Beliefs of the SDA Church — a comprehensive statement of biblical faith covering God, salvation, the church, and Christian living.

Explore All 28 Beliefs
01

Holy Scriptures

The Bible — 66 books — is the inspired, authoritative Word of God and the only rule of faith and practice.

05

The Trinity

One God in three co-eternal persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — perfectly united in nature, character, and purpose.

10

Salvation in Christ

In His infinite love, God gave Jesus to save fallen humanity. By His grace, through faith alone, we are justified and restored.

20

The Sabbath

Saturday — the seventh day — is God's holy day of rest, given at creation and reaffirmed throughout Scripture as a sign of relationship with our Creator.

24

The Second Coming

Christ's literal, personal, visible, and glorious return is the blessed hope — the grand climax of the gospel and the Christian's ultimate expectation.

22

Christian Stewardship

God is owner of all things. We are stewards of time, talents, and resources — returning tithe and offerings in joyful gratitude and trust.

14

Unity in the Body

In Christ, all barriers of race, class, or gender are broken down. The church is one body — diverse in expression, united in mission.

28

The New Earth

After the millennium, God will re-create the earth as the eternal home of the redeemed — where sin, sorrow, and death are no more.
