Who We Are

A community shaped
by grace.

CukSDA is the official Seventh-day Adventist student and staff community at the Cooperative University of Kenya, Karen Campus — a place where faith is lived, not just professed.

We are a multigenerational, multicultural fellowship — students, faculty, staff, and friends — all walking together in faith. Whether you are a lifelong Adventist or simply curious about Christianity, you are deeply welcome here.

Our life together is built around the Sabbath experience — from Sabbath School at 9:00 AM to the Divine Service at 11:00 AM — and extends through the week with prayer meetings, Bible studies, outreach, and ministry projects that touch every corner of the campus.

Purpose & Direction

Why we exist and
where we're going.

Our Vision

A transformed campus community reflecting Christ.

We envision every student and staff member at CUK having a genuine, personal encounter with Jesus Christ — and a faith community strong enough to sustain that journey through university life and beyond. We believe God placed this campus in our hearts because He intends to use it as a mission field.

Our Mission

Making disciples who worship, grow, and serve.

CukSDA exists to make and mature disciples of Jesus Christ within the CUK community — through authentic worship, intentional discipleship, compassionate service, and bold Spirit-led evangelism to all people, on campus and beyond.

Our Commitment

Scripture-rooted, Spirit-led, community-driven.

Everything we do is grounded in the inspired Word of God, empowered by the Holy Spirit, and lived out in the context of a loving, accountable, diverse church family. We are proudly part of the global Seventh-day Adventist Church and the East Kenya Union Conference.

On Campus
2008

The Seed Is Planted

A handful of Adventist students at CUK began meeting informally for prayer and Bible study in a dormitory common room. These early gatherings — just five people and a worn Bible — laid the spiritual foundation for what would become a thriving campus church.

🌱 Foundation
2011

Official Recognition & First Sabbath Services

CukSDA was officially registered as a campus ministry and recognised by both CUK administration and the East Kenya Union Conference of Seventh-day Adventists. Sabbath services moved into the Main Hall for the first time, with the community growing to nearly 60 members.

✦ Milestone
2014

Ministry Expansion — AY, Health & Music

The Ambassadors for Christ Youth (AY) Society, the Health & Wellness Ministry, and the Music Department were formally established — enabling CukSDA to serve students not just spiritually but in body and creativity too. Membership crossed 100 for the first time.

✦ Milestone
2017

Our First Campus Evangelistic Crusade

CukSDA hosted its inaugural week-long campus evangelistic series in partnership with the East Kenya Union. The series saw over 40 students give their hearts to Christ and be baptised in the same week — a landmark moment that affirmed God's hand on this community and sparked our annual tradition.

🙌 40+ Baptisms
2020

Resilience Through a Global Pandemic

When COVID-19 disrupted in-person gatherings worldwide, CukSDA pivoted rapidly to online Sabbath services, virtual prayer nights, digital Bible study groups, and pastoral care via WhatsApp. Our community stayed connected and spiritually alive through one of the most challenging periods in modern history — proof that our fellowship transcends four walls.

💪 Resilience
2022

Health Ministry: Serving 300+ Lives

Relaunching the Health Ministry in full force, CukSDA partnered with local hospitals and health professionals to offer free blood pressure, blood sugar, and BMI screenings, nutrition workshops, and mental health support for the CUK community — serving over 300 people in its debut year alone.

💚 300+ Served
Today · 2026

Growing Stronger Together

With over 200 active members, eight thriving ministries, a packed annual events calendar, and a heart for the whole CUK campus — CukSDA continues to grow in depth and breadth. The best, we believe, is still ahead. Come be part of it.

Core Values

What shapes everything we do.

These are not words on a wall — they are the convictions that guide how we worship, relate, serve, and grow together as a campus church family.

"Faith without works is dead."

James 2:26
01 — Scripture First

The Bible Is Our Authority

We read it, preach it, study it, and live it — believing every word is God-breathed and our final guide in all matters of faith and life.

02 — Authentic Worship

Whole-Life Response to God

We gather on the Sabbath with reverence, joy, and expectation — offering God the best of who we are in Spirit and in truth.

03 — Genuine Community

Nobody Walks Alone

We build deep, honest, caring relationships — a family where every person is known, loved, and belongs from day one, regardless of background.

04 — Bold Evangelism

Sharing the Best News

The gospel is the best news the world has ever heard. We share it boldly, compassionately, and creatively — on campus, in Karen, and beyond Kenya.

05 — Intentional Discipleship

Faith Grows in Relationship

We invest in mentorship, Bible study, and one-on-one care — because we are called to make disciples who make disciples, not just attendees.

06 — Wholistic Health

Body, Mind & Spirit

We champion total wellbeing — physical health, emotional resilience, and spiritual wholeness — because God cares about every dimension of who we are.

What We Believe

Anchored in Scripture,
united in truth.

As a Seventh-day Adventist congregation, our beliefs are drawn from the 28 Fundamental Beliefs of the SDA Church — a comprehensive statement of biblical faith covering God, salvation, the church, and Christian living. These are not rules to follow but truths to live by.

"Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path."

Psalm 119:105
Explore All 28 Beliefs
Belief 01

Holy Scriptures

The 66 books of the Bible are the inspired, authoritative Word of God and the only rule of faith and practice.

Belief 05

The Trinity

One God in three co-eternal persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — perfectly united in nature, character, and purpose.

Belief 10

Salvation in Christ

In infinite love, God gave Jesus to save fallen humanity. By grace through faith alone, we are justified and restored.

Belief 20

The Sabbath

Saturday — the seventh day — is God's holy day of rest, given at creation as a sign of relationship with our Creator.

Belief 24

The Second Coming

Christ's literal, personal, visible return is the blessed hope — the grand climax of the gospel and the Christian's ultimate expectation.

Belief 22

Christian Stewardship

God is owner of all things. We are stewards of time, talents, and resources — returning tithe and offerings with joy.

Belief 14

Unity in the Body

In Christ, all barriers are broken. The church is one body — diverse in expression, united in mission and love.

Belief 28

The New Earth

God will re-create the earth as the eternal home of the redeemed — where sin, sorrow, and death are no more.
