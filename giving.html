Our Philosophy

The Heart of Biblical Stewardship

At CukSDA, we view giving not as a financial transaction, but as a deeply spiritual act of worship and trust in God's providence.

We recognize that everything we have—our time, talents, and resources—are gifts entrusted to us by God. Faithful stewardship is our response to His unending grace.

As a campus church, every shilling given directly supports evangelism, nurtures spiritual growth among students, and cares for the physical needs of our community through our welfare programs.

Understanding Your Giving

Where does the money go?

As a Seventh-day Adventist Church, we follow a globally recognized, transparent system for managing the funds entrusted to us.

Tithe (10%)

The tithe is strictly reserved for the gospel ministry. 100% of the tithe collected at CukSDA is forwarded to the local Conference to support pastors, evangelists, and global missions. It is not used for local church expenses.

Local Church Budget

Your free-will offerings sustain our campus ministry. This fund pays for equipment, Sabbath school materials, venue maintenance, guest speakers, and day-to-day operational costs of the church.

Welfare & Dorcas

Specifically designated to help students in financial distress, provide meals for the less fortunate around the university, and support community outreach initiatives spearheaded by the Adventist Community Services.

Special Projects (e.g. Camp Meeting)

These are seasonal or goal-specific funds. For example, contributing towards the annual Camp Meeting expenses, acquiring a new church keyboard, or funding a major evangelistic crusade.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I get a receipt for my M-Pesa giving?

If you give via M-Pesa, the church treasury receives an automatic notification. To ensure you get an official church receipt, please present your M-Pesa confirmation message to the treasury desk immediately after the Sabbath service.

What is the difference between Tithe and Offering?

The tithe is a specific, biblical mandate to return 10% of our income to God, which is used exclusively to support the global pastoral ministry. Offerings are free-will gifts given above and beyond the tithe, and they are used to support local church operations, departments, and community outreach.

Can I direct my offering to a specific department?

Yes. If you are using a physical envelope, simply write the amount next to the specific department (e.g., Youth, Choir, Welfare). If giving via M-Pesa, please use the Account Name section to specify (e.g., "Offering - Choir"). If unspecified, it goes to the general Combined Church Budget.

Is my financial information secure?

Absolutely. We uphold strict confidentiality regarding all personal giving records. Only the treasury department handles your financial records, and they do so with the highest level of integrity and privacy in accordance with church policy.
