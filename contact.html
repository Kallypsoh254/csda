Getting Here

How to find us.

Karen Campus is well connected. Here are the easiest ways to get to CukSDA on Sabbath morning.

By Car / Boda Boda

1
Head towards Karen along Ngong Road or Karen Road.
2
Turn onto Lang'ata Road — CUK Karen Campus is signposted from the main junction.
3
Enter through the main gate — inform security you are attending the SDA church service.
4
Free parking is available within the campus grounds on Saturdays.

By Matatu / Public Bus

1
From the CBD, board any matatu headed to Karen or Lang'ata (Route 111, 126, or 125).
2
Alight at Karen Shopping Centre or the Hardy Junction stage.
3
Take a boda boda or tuk-tuk to Cooperative University of Kenya (about 10–15 min).
4
Proceed to the main gate and direct the guard to the SDA church inside campus.

On Campus (Students)

1
From CUK student hostels, walk towards the main academic block — follow signage for the chapel area.
2
The CukSDA Hall is adjacent to the main multipurpose block — ask any SDA student on Sabbath morning.
3
WhatsApp our welcome team for a guide to walk with you: +254 700 000 000.

Common Questions

Questions before you visit?

Do I have to be a Seventh-day Adventist to attend?

Not at all. Everyone is genuinely welcome — whether you're a first-time visitor, someone exploring faith for the first time, or a Christian from another denomination. There is no pressure, no commitment required. Come as you are.

What should I wear to the service?

Smart casual to formal is the norm — many members dress in their best for Sabbath as a sign of reverence. But we won't turn you away for wearing a T-shirt and jeans. What matters most is that you come.

Is parking available on campus?

Yes. Free parking is available within the CUK Karen Campus grounds every Saturday. Inform the security guard at the main gate that you are attending the SDA church service.

Is there a service for children?

Yes. Children are warmly welcome at all services. We have a dedicated Sabbath School class for children during the Sabbath School hour (9–10:30 AM) and a children's corner within the main service. Nursing mothers also have a comfortable side room available.

How do I transfer membership to CukSDA?

Speak to our Church Clerk, Sis. G. Wanjiku, after any service or email admin@cuksda.org. You'll need a letter of transfer from your home church. The process is straightforward and the team will guide you through every step.

Can non-students attend CukSDA services?

Absolutely. While we're based at a university, CukSDA is open to the entire Karen and Nairobi community — staff, families, and community members are all welcome. Our congregation includes people from many walks of life.

Do you accept tithe and offering online?

Yes. You can give via M-Pesa Paybill Number 123456, Account: CUKSDA TITHE or CUKSDA OFFERING. Offering envelopes are also available in the church foyer every Sabbath.
